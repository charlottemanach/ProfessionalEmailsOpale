Key-elements of a professional email

In order to maintain a professional standard in your emails, you need to follow a specific structure.

1. Informative subject line:

Mentionner clairement l’objet du mail : “Information request” or “Request for an appointment with Paul”.

2: Greeting (or Opening)

"To Whom It May Concern:" ; “Dear Sir/Madam”­ if you don’t know their name

"Dear Sarah,"

"Dear Mrs. Price," ­ more formal

"Hi Kelly," ­ less formal

Hello [Recipient's Name],

Good [morning/afternoon/evening] [Recipient's Name],

3. Polite inquiry about the recipient’s well-being (optional but very common):

I hope this email finds you well.

I hope you're having a great day so far!

4. Body

a) Reference / Thanking the recipient:

With reference to our meeting / phone call / With reference to your e-mail dated 12/10/2023,…

Following our meeting on…

"Thank you for contacting ABC Company." ­ replying to an e­mail

"Thank you for your prompt reply." or "Thanks for getting back to me." ­ if they responded to an e­mail you sent.

b) Aim of the e-mail

I am contacting you to inform you that…

I am writing to you to…

"I am writing in reference to/connection with …"

c) Announcing good news

I am pleased to inform you that…

I am delighted to let you know that…

You will be pleased to hear that…

d) Announcing bad news

I regret to inform you that… / Unfortunately, …

We apologise for… Nous vous prions de nous excuser pour…

5. Asking for something

Could you please…?

I would appreciate it if you could…

I would be grateful if you could… send me your reply by COB today (=Close of Business= End of the Day) -> Less formal: “I’d appreciate a reply asap.”

6. Attachments

Please find attached … (e-mail)

7. Closing remarks

For more details, please contact…

If you require more information, you can visit our website at…

I look forward to hearing from you/meeting you/speaking with you.

I’m looking forward to your reply/comments/visit, etc.

Thank you for your patience and cooperation. / Thank you for your consideration.

If you have any questions or concerns, don't hesitate to let me know.

8. Salutations

Yours faithfully / sincerely, /truly / respectfully: Veuillez agréer l’expression de mes salutations distinguées (formel)

Sincerely yours : très cordialement

Regards / With regards / Best regards, Best,

Kind regards, Kindest regards, Warm regards -> plus chaleureux, si vous avez déjà une bonne relation.

N'oubliez pas les mots de liaison !

Aidez-vous des mots de liaison pour structurer vos différents paragraphes : « First », « To begin with », « Also », « Then », « Finally », « To conclude », « In conclusion ».
